Dear students, almost-artists, amateurs, sympathisers... Because you chose art education, chances are you are visual, image-minded people. In this course we focus on new ways to expand your own artistic toolbox.

What you will learn
  • Lab44 as a place where art education and technology meet
  • Why computational thinking remains essential in the AI era
  • Two quotes that set the tone: Carl Sagan and Barack Obama

Lab44

The activities inside the lab - Lab44 - tie closely to the daily technological changes and innovations we as teachers, and you as students, are confronted with. Looking at what the future will bring and which skills will be most necessary for our students is a bit like reading tea leaves.

What the lab can do, however, is teach the skills needed to engage with these new technologies flexibly and without fear, and to embrace them.

Lab44 wants to play a pioneering role in using new applications and principles for the personal growth of students - both their skills and their personality.

Foreword

Yes, you will meet all the concepts, functions and structures that teach you to think "computationally" - but from the deep confidence that your learning will flow smoothly, surfing the waves of the visual output you quickly receive back.

"We live in a society exquisitely dependent on science and technology, in which hardly anyone knows anything about science and technology."

- Carl Sagan

The recent shift toward artificial intelligence makes these words more relevant than ever. AI can automate tasks, discover connections and even propose creative ideas, but those who understand the language of code look beyond the black box. Programming literacy gives insight into the mechanisms behind AI, enables critical thinking, and opens the door to building and experimenting yourself.

In a time when technology is everywhere, coding remains not only useful but essential to preserve our autonomy, creativity and curiosity.

"Everybody's got to learn how to code early."

- Barack Obama
