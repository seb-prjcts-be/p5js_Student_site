There are dozens of AI models available. Which model is strongest at reasoning? Which is best for code? And what about European alternatives? This is a comparative overview.

How we compared

For this overview, each model was tested with the same prompt:

"I'm a student in a creative program. I have an idea for an interactive installation where visuals respond to sound. Can you help me realize this?"

Each answer was evaluated on four criteria:

  • Understanding of the question - does the model grasp what is being asked?
  • Creativity - does the model offer original or surprising ideas?
  • Technical depth - are concrete tools, code, or approaches suggested?
  • Clarity - is the answer readable and usable?

Score per criterion: 1–10.

European AI: Mistral and Le Chat

Not all strong AI models come from the US. Mistral AI is a French company that develops open-source and commercial models that come close to GPT-4 in performance.

Mistral

  • Open-source models available via Hugging Face
  • Lighter and faster than GPT-4 for simpler tasks
  • Popular with developers who want to run models locally

Le Chat

Le Chat is Mistral's own chat interface - comparable to ChatGPT but from Europe. Available via lechat.mistral.ai.

  • Falls under European legislation (GDPR)
  • No training on your conversations without consent
  • Free basic version available

Important note about EU models

If you process sensitive information - personal data, student work, confidential ideas - then it's relevant where the servers that process your data are located.

  • American models (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) are processed on servers in the US, under American law
  • European models (Mistral, Le Chat) are processed in Europe, under the GDPR
  • For schoolwork and personal experiments, this rarely makes a practical difference
  • For professional or sensitive applications, this is worth considering

No single model is perfect for all situations. Try multiple models for the same problem - you'll be surprised by the differences.
