There are dozens of AI models available, but which ones can you as a student use for free? And what do you actually get? This overview compares the most important AI chatbots based on their free offering as of April 2026.

Copilot (Microsoft)

Maker: Microsoft (US) - uses OpenAI models

Free model: GPT-5 (automatic, no choice needed)

Copilot is your most obvious option: everyone has a Microsoft 365 school account, so you get immediate access without extra registration.

What you get with your school account

  • Runs on GPT-5 - the same model as in the paid version
  • Web search built in by default (up-to-date information)
  • No aggressive limits - works fine for daily use
  • Extra privacy protection: your data is processed within the school license framework (FERPA/COPPA)
  • Available via copilot.microsoft.com, Windows 11, Edge and the Microsoft 365 apps

Copilot Chat vs. Copilot in Microsoft 365

Copilot Chat is included in your school license - this is the chatbot you can ask questions, have texts written and have code explained. The extended version (Copilot for Microsoft 365) with AI directly in Word, Excel, PowerPoint and Teams is a paid add-on that the school must purchase separately.

ChatGPT (OpenAI)

Maker: OpenAI (San Francisco, US)

Free model: GPT-5.3 Instant (falls back to GPT-5.3 Mini during heavy use)

ChatGPT is the most well-known AI model in the world and remains the standard that other models are compared to.

Free version

  • ~10 messages per 5 hours with GPT-5.3, then automatically a lighter model
  • Web search, voice interaction and file uploads included
  • Shows ads in the US (since February 2026)
  • Available via chatgpt.com

Strong at

  • Broadly applicable: text, code, analysis, image generation
  • Largest ecosystem: GPTs, plugins, API
  • Strong in technical elaboration and code

Gemini (Google)

Maker: Google DeepMind (US)

Free model: Gemini 3 Flash (default in the Gemini app)

Gemini is Google's AI assistant, integrated into search, Gmail and Google Workspace.

Free version

  • Gemini 3 Flash as default model - fast, capable answers
  • Gemini 3.1 Pro and Thinking models only with subscription
  • Available via gemini.google.com
  • In some countries free upgrade for students 18+ (including UK, Japan - not Belgium/Netherlands)

Strong at

  • Multimodal: text, image, audio, video as input
  • Integration with Google products (Docs, Sheets, Gmail)
  • Large context window: up to 1 million tokens in paid version

Claude (Anthropic)

Maker: Anthropic (San Francisco, US)

Free model: Claude Sonnet 4.5

Claude stands out through accurate reasoning, long documents and a clear writing style. It hallucinates less than many competitors.

Free version

  • ~15-40 messages per 5 hours (depending on complexity and server load)
  • 200K token context window - entire books or long code in one conversation
  • Projects, artifacts, web search and file uploads included
  • Claude Opus (the strongest model) only for paying users
  • Available via claude.ai

Strong at

  • Analyzing and summarizing long texts
  • Clear and nuanced writing
  • Writing and explaining code

Grok (xAI)

Maker: xAI (Elon Musk, US)

Free model: Grok (basic version)

Grok is the AI model from xAI, integrated into X (formerly Twitter). It has real-time access to posts on the platform.

Free version

  • ~10 prompts per 2 hours - the strictest limit of all free options
  • Access to basic models, not the latest top model
  • Available via grok.com and the X app

Strong at

  • Up-to-date information via X/Twitter
  • Less censored than competitors

Mistral & Le Chat (Europe)

Maker: Mistral AI (Paris, France)

Free model: Mistral Medium/Small via Le Chat

Mistral is the most important European alternative. Le Chat is their chat interface - comparable to ChatGPT but from Europe.

Free version

  • ~25 messages per day
  • Web search, code interpreter and document uploads included
  • Available via chat.mistral.ai

Strong at

  • European privacy: servers in the EU, under the GDPR
  • No training on your conversations without consent
  • Cheapest paid option: Le Chat Pro costs only $14.99/month
  • Open-source models available for local use

Which AI as a student?

You all have a Microsoft 365 account, so Copilot is your starting point. But don't limit yourself to just that - each model has different strengths:

  • Copilot - always available via your school account, no registration needed, generous limits
  • Gemini - strong if you're already in the Google ecosystem (Chromebook, Google Docs). Generous free version.
  • ChatGPT - the most versatile model, but the free limit is tight. Good as a supplement.
  • Claude - best writing quality and code explanation. Handy for longer projects thanks to the large context window.

Tip: use multiple AIs side by side. Each model has different strengths. Ask the same question to two or three models and compare the answers - that way you learn their differences and get better results.

Privacy and European legislation

If you process sensitive information - personal data, student work, confidential ideas - it's relevant where the servers are located.

  • American models (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot, Grok) process data on servers in the US
  • Mistral / Le Chat processes data in Europe, under the GDPR
  • For schoolwork and personal experiments, this rarely makes a practical difference
  • Copilot via a school account (M365 Education) offers extra privacy protection under FERPA/COPPA

Never share personal data, passwords or private photos with an AI chatbot - regardless of which model you use.
