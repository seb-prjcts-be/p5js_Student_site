Here you will find all the links and resources from the course - from inspiring projects to books, videos, tools, and datasets. Use this list to explore further, learn, and experiment.

Inspiring projects

  • Listen to Wikipedia - Hatnote
    Every edit on Wikipedia is converted into a tone in real time. A minimalist, meditative experience.
  • Conductor / MTA.ME - Alexander Chen
    The New York City subway map as an interactive string instrument.
  • Land Lines - Zach Lieberman & Matt Felsen
    Satellite imagery from Google Maps linked to gesture recognition. Draw a line and find it back in the landscape.
  • Audience (2008) - Random International
    An installation of hundreds of small mirrors that respond to visitors - like a swarm that follows you. Technical overview by Chris O'Shea.

  • Everything in Existence - fuse* studio
    An immersive solo exhibition that merges data, imagery, and space.
  • Technicontrol - GMUNK (Bradley Munkowitz)
    Robotics, code, and screen content combined into spectacular motion design.
  • XYZT: Abstract Landscapes - Adrien M & Claire B
    Interactive installations that visualize abstract mathematical landscapes.
  • Bring Your Own Beamer - Nerdlab Ghent
    An event where everyone brings their own projector and fills a space with generative art. (Aftermovie 2022)
  • Tim Rodenbroker
    Creative coder and founder of the DEMO festival. Many tutorials and projects on typography and generative design.
  • Fionn Breen
    Creative coder with a keen eye for movement and systems. An inspiring portfolio of generative works.
  • Tendrils - Google Experiments
    Procedural drawing with flowing tentacles that follow your mouse. An elegant demo of how little code can produce great beauty.
  • Scan Sequence - Jeongho Park
    Interactive generative work where lines and patterns glide across the screen like a scanner.

John Whitney - harmonic motion

John Whitney (1917-1995) is the founder of computer-generated animation. In the 1960s he made films by moving points based on sine waves with different frequencies. The result: abstract patterns that seem to breathe. Try it yourself:

Videos & documentaries

  • John Whitney - Experiments in Motion Graphics (1968)
    One of the first computer-generated animations ever made. Timeless and impressive.
  • Zoom into the Mandelbrot fractal - factor 10^227
    An endless zoom that shows what mathematical recursion looks like visually.
  • Clouds Are Not Spheres - Netflix (trailer)
    Documentary about Benoit Mandelbrot and the discovery of fractals.
  • Sonification of Meteorite Landings
    A p5.js project that translates NASA meteorite data into visuals and sound.

    Ten minutes of live coding: building a p5.js sketch from scratch for an audience.

    The Monty Python actor and writer on how creativity works, what blocks it, and how you can consciously cultivate it. A classic that remains just as relevant today.

Learning & community

  • The Coding Train - Daniel Shiffman
    The best place to learn p5.js through hundreds of videos. Enthusiastic, funny, and thorough. Also on YouTube and in the Discord community.
  • Patt Vira - YouTube
    Clear and visually appealing tutorials on creative coding with p5.js.
  • p5.js Web Editor
    Write and share p5.js sketches in the browser, no installation needed.
  • p5.js Reference
    The official documentation of all functions, variables, and classes in p5.js 2.x.
  • Awesome Creative Coding - Terkel Gjervig
    An extensive, community-maintained list of tools, libraries, books, courses, and communities around creative coding.

Books

  • The Nature of Code (2nd edition) - Daniel Shiffman
    Free online book about simulations, forces, particles, neural networks - all in p5.js.
  • Generative Gestaltung - Gross, Bohnacker, Laub, Lazzeroni
    The standard work on generative design with p5.js. All code examples are free to download.
  • Getting Started with p5.js - Lauren McCarthy, Casey Reas & Ben Fry
    The official beginner's book by the creators of p5.js themselves.

p5.js libraries

Extensions that add new capabilities to p5.js:

  • p5.Capture
    Record your sketch as video (MP4, GIF, WebM) with a single line of code.
  • p5.play
    Add physics, gravity, and collisions to your sketch - ideal for games.
  • p5.speech
    Speech recognition and text-to-speech in the browser via p5.js.

Data & APIs

Sources for interesting datasets to visualize:

  • Corpora
    Collection of hundreds of fun and useful JSON files: animal names, colors, countries, mythology, ... Also available via the Corpora API.
  • Open Data City of Ghent
    CSV, JSON, and APIs about Ghent: mobility, culture, environment, and more.
  • Open Data Belgium
    Official Belgian open data portal with thousands of datasets.
  • NASA Open Data
    Space data, satellite imagery, planets, meteorite landings - all freely accessible. Also check the Meteorite Landings API as a concrete starting point.
