Everything comes together in your final project. You've gotten to know AI as an explainer, debugger, code generator and creative tool. Now it's your turn: make something where AI plays a conscious part - and show what you brought to it.

The assignment

Create a creative work in p5.js where AI plays a conscious role. This can be done in different ways:

  • AI as medium - Use ml5.js to make your sketch react to body, sound or image
  • AI as co-author - Let AI generate code that you then adapt and make your own
  • AI as subject - Create a work about AI: what it can do, what it can't, how it influences us
  • AI as process - Document how you worked iteratively with AI to reach a result

The form is free: an interactive sketch, an installation, a visual story, a generative artwork - as long as you can explain what role AI plays and which choices are yours.

Optional starter projects

No idea yet? These are possible starting points - not required. Pick one or design your own project.

  • Halftone webcam - Turn your webcam image into a grid of circles (or other shapes) based on brightness. Small project, big effect. Works well with iterative AI help per step: read the image, measure greyscale values, draw shapes, set a threshold.
  • Body-paint - Use BodyPose or HandPose (ml5.js) to draw with your body. The body becomes the brush.
  • Generative portrait - Combine FaceMesh with your own aesthetic: replace the face image with shapes, lines or colour fields that respond to your facial features.
  • Sound-reactive image - Make a sketch that reacts to your voice or music via SoundClassifier or an FFT analysis.
  • AI as subject - Make a sketch that is about AI: about data centres, training data, bias, or the ecological cost. Image as commentary.

Project setup

A good project starts with a clear concept. Answer these questions before you start coding:

  1. What do I want to make? - Describe your idea in two sentences.
  2. What role does AI play? - Is it a tool, a co-creator, a subject?
  3. What is my own contribution? - What do you add that the AI cannot?
  4. What do I need? - Which technologies, models or tools will I use?
  5. How do I show the result? - Screen, installation, video, live demo?

Working time

Plan your time realistically. Divide your work into manageable steps:

Phase 1: concept

  • Develop and discuss your idea
  • Collect references (existing artworks, example code)
  • Check technical feasibility: does the model you want to use work?

Phase 2: build

  • Create basic version - focus on functionality, not aesthetics
  • Test AI integration
  • Iterate: refine, adjust, improve

Phase 3: finish

  • Visual refinement: colours, composition, timing
  • Handle edge cases: what if the webcam doesn't work? What if there's no sound?
  • Prepare presentation

Presenting

You present your final work to the class. A good presentation shows not only the result, but also the process.

Structure for your presentation

  1. Show your work - Demo or video of the final result
  2. Tell what AI did - Which AI tools or models did you use? What did the AI generate?
  3. Tell what you did - Which choices did you make yourself? What did you adjust?
  4. Show the process - Show intermediate versions. How did it evolve?
  5. Reflect - What did you learn? What would you do differently?

Tips

  • Save screenshots of every iteration
  • Save your prompts - they tell the story of your collaboration with AI
  • Be honest about what did and didn't work
  • Practice your demo beforehand - technology can fail unexpectedly

Documenting AI use

In academic work you cite every source you use. The same applies to AI: if you worked with AI, note which model you used, when, and what your prompt was. This isn't a formality - it makes your work honest and reproducible, and it helps you see later how an idea grew.

You do this in the code itself, using // comments right above the block that was influenced by AI. No separate document, no side text file - right next to the code it's about, so context and source always travel together.

Minimum citation

For every AI contribution in your project, note at least:

  • Model - e.g. ChatGPT-5, Claude Sonnet 4.6, Gemini 2.5
  • Date - when you had the conversation (AI answers change over time)
  • Prompt - the question you asked, verbatim
  • What you did with it - typed out, adapted, ignored, or used only as inspiration

Example

// AI source
// Model: ChatGPT-5 (free tier)
// Date: 2026-04-17
// Prompt: "How do I make a halftone effect from a webcam
//          image in p5.js, using circles instead of pixels?"
// Used: base idea typed out, threshold and colour palette
//       adjusted myself.

function draw() {
  // your code
}

You can refer to these comment blocks in your presentation.

Why this matters

  • Honesty - you don't claim something as fully your own when AI contributed
  • Learning - saving your prompts shows you which questions gave good answers
  • Reproducibility - someone else can understand and retrace your process
  • Critical distance - documenting forces you to choose consciously instead of passively accepting

Reflection

After the presentations, it's time to look back. Not just at your own work, but at the entire journey.

Questions to think about

  • When was AI most useful to you? When did it get in the way?
  • How has your image of AI changed compared to the beginning?
  • Can you feel the difference between something the AI came up with and something that came from you?
  • Would you continue using AI in your creative work in the future? How?
  • What does "authorship" mean when AI collaborates?

The core

AI is a tool - an extraordinarily powerful tool, but still a tool. The value lies not in what the AI can do, but in what you do with it. Your ideas, your choices, your aesthetics - that's what makes the difference between generated content and creative work.

Assessment criteria

Criterion Description
Concept Is the idea clear and original? Does it fit the theme of AI & creativity?
AI integration Is AI used in a conscious and meaningful way?
Own contribution Are there clear personal choices? Is the work more than just AI output?
Technical execution Does the sketch work? Is the code well-maintained?
Presentation Can you explain what you made, why, and what the role of AI was?
Process Is the iterative process visible? Are intermediate versions saved?
AI attribution Are the models, dates and prompts used documented? Is it clear what AI contributed?
